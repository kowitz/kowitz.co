# Approach

Four themes run through Braden's design leadership philosophy:

## Product Discovery
Braden is passionate about helping cross-functional teams build a real process of product discovery — systematically exploring an opportunity-solution space through user research, prototyping, and in-market tests. This is what the Design Sprint method is built around, but discovery is a continuous practice, not a one-week event.

## Effective Teams
Building healthy teams requires attention to fundamentals: trust and vulnerability, helping people get in the groove so they can focus on what they do best, and creating opportunities for growth. Team culture compounds — small investments in psychological safety and clarity pay back enormously over time.

## Cross-collaboration
Braden doesn't believe in design for its own sake. His approach is cross-functional collaboration, bringing design methods to bear on the key business challenges — whether that's in marketing, product, strategy, or something else entirely. Design is a lens on problems, not a separate workstream.

## Craft & Quality
Design has a key role within organizations to lead the way toward a culture of quality. That means leaders setting a very high bar, and the whole team building their skillsets of critique, analysis, and exploration. Quality isn't an aesthetic concern; it's a strategic one.
